Smart AC Unit

I led a team of three in designing and prototyping an innovative "Smart AC Unit" device that enables users to remotely control and automate the operation of a window-mounted air conditioning unit through a mobile application. Our system integrated two high-precision SHT30 temperature and humidity sensors to accurately calculate the room's heat index within ±0.2881°C. An ESP32-S3 microcontroller served as the central control unit, interfacing with the sensors, a user-friendly Android app via WiFi, and a pair of high-torque MS24 servo motors to physically manipulate the cooling and temperature knobs on a 5000 BTU Arctic King AC unit. Key accomplishments included developing a robust 3D-printed actuator mount to reliably turn the analog knobs, programming the ESP32 with heat index calculations and logic to adjust settings based on the user's scheduled temperatures in the app, and overcoming challenges like managing heat dispersion that caused sensor inaccuracies. While a damaged display unit prevented showing the temperature on the device itself, our final prototype successfully demonstrated wireless monitoring and automated control of the AC settings for improved energy efficiency and user convenience. This intensive project showcased my skills in embedded systems design, PCB layout, mobile app development using Android Studio, and the full-stack integration of hardware components with software across firmware, networking, and a user interface. Leading this capstone highlighted my abilities in system architecture, team collaboration, and delivering a functional IoT product from concept to implementation.
